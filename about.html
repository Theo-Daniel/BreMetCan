What is BreMetCan ?

BreMetCan is a database for studying the effects of human breast cancer metastasis.

Here, we break down the data in terms of substance and scope, making it easier for you to access what you'll find in BreMetCan and how to get the most out of it for your research needs.

Overview

BreMetCan is a database that comprises of data from the COSMIC - Catalogue of Somatic Mutations in cancer, HCMDB (Human Cancer Metastasis Database) and UniprotKB.


Sections Included:


  1. Protein Description: This includes the Protein id, name function etc.
  2. Post transcriptional Modification: These are the changes that occur to a newly transcribed
    primary RNA transcript (hnRNA) after transcription has occurred and prior to its translation
    into a protein product.
  3. Phosphorylation data: Phosphorylation is a post-translational modification of proteins in
    which an amino acid residue is phosphorylated by a protein kinase by the addition of a
    covalently bound phosphate group.
  4. Mutations: Changes in the sequence of the original protein.
  5. miRNA Involved
  6. lncRNA involved
  7. Small molecules or drugs inhibiting the protein
